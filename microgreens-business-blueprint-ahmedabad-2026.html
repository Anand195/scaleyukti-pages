Risk Management

Crop Risks, Prevention & Early Diagnostics

Ahmedabad's heat and humidity make mold the #1 threat. Know the signs, prevent before they happen.

HIGH RISK
Mold / Fungal Growth
Overwatering + poor air circulation = white/grey fuzzy mold. Ahmedabad humidity in monsoon season (Jun–Sep) makes this your biggest ongoing threat. Can ruin entire tray in 24 hours.
Prevention Always use bottom watering after germination. Run fan 4–6 hrs/day. Maintain 50–60% humidity (use hygrometer). Sterilize trays with diluted hydrogen peroxide (3%) before each grow. Space trays — don't overcrowd shelves.
HIGH RISK
Heat Stress
Ahmedabad reaches 44°C+ in April–June. Microgreens need 18–25°C. Anything above 30°C causes rapid wilting, stunted growth, and poor yield. Monsoon humidity (75–85%) compounds the problem.
Prevention Grow in AC room set to 22–24°C during summer. Use a cooler or exhaust fan during shoulder months. Never place near a window getting direct afternoon sun. In winter (Nov–Feb) Ahmedabad is ideal — no AC needed.
MEDIUM RISK
Damping Off (Base Rot)
Fungal disease at soil/stem junction causing seedlings to collapse at base. Common with overwatering and unsterilized growing medium. Spreads fast tray-to-tray.
Prevention Only use coco peat (not soil) — coco peat has natural antifungal properties. Always sterilize trays between batches. Reduce watering frequency if you see stem darkening near base.
MEDIUM RISK
Leggy / Etiolated Growth
Microgreens growing tall, thin, and pale with weak stems. Caused by insufficient light. Looks fine but has poor nutritional density and wilts quickly after harvest — bad for customer experience.
Prevention Use LED full-spectrum grow lights 12–16 hrs/day. Keep lights 15–20cm above tray surface. Natural sunlight from window is NOT enough in most Indian homes — LED lights are non-negotiable for consistent quality.
MEDIUM RISK
Poor / Uneven Germination
Seeds not sprouting, or only 50% germination rate. Causes: old/low-quality seeds, incorrect seed density, growing medium too dry/wet during blackout phase.
Prevention Always buy seeds from reputable suppliers (IndiaMart/Urja Seeds/Organicbazar). Store seeds in airtight container in cool dry place. Test germination rate (50 seeds on wet paper towel) before bulk planting. Maintain 70% moisture during 2–3 day blackout phase.
LOW RISK
Pest Infestation
Fungus gnats, aphids, or spider mites. Less common in indoor setups but possible if growing medium has soil or if there are houseplants nearby. Can damage crops if unchecked.
Prevention Use only coco peat (not soil) — prevents fungus gnats. Keep growing area clean — no food scraps nearby. Yellow sticky traps catch flying pests early. Neem oil spray (diluted) as preventive measure monthly.

🔍 Early Diagnostic Signs — What to Look For Daily

🤍
White fluffy threads on seeds (days 1–3)
✅ NORMAL — these are root hairs, not mold. Root hairs grow from seed tips and disappear after watering. Mold smells musty; root hairs don't.
🟡
Yellow leaves after sprouting
⚠️ ACTION NEEDED — either overwatering (reduce watering, check drainage holes) OR insufficient light (move closer to LED, add grow hours).
🟤
Brown tips on leaves
⚠️ ACTION NEEDED — heat stress (room too hot, above 28°C) OR low humidity (below 40%). Check thermometer/hygrometer immediately.
🔵
Stems collapsing at base
🚨 URGENT — damping off fungus. Isolate that tray immediately. Do NOT water it. Increase airflow. If spreading, discard tray, sterilize shelf with H2O2.
🟢
Slow, uniform growth (all trays same speed)
✅ NORMAL — consistent growth is a sign of stable environment. If one tray is much slower, it may have poor seeds or less light exposure — rotate trays.
⬜
Grey/black patches on growing medium
🚨 MOLD — not root hairs. Smells musty. Spray diluted hydrogen peroxide (1:10 with water) immediately. Reduce watering. Increase fan speed.
